Set in academia, this novel follows a PhD student who enters a fake relationship with a notoriously intimidating professor. Our review finds it a delightful blend of humor, slow-burn tension, and emotional sincerity.

## Why we recommend it

The STEM setting adds freshness to a familiar trope, while the characters’ insecurities and ambitions make them relatable. The romance develops gradually, emphasizing trust and vulnerability.

## Key takeaways

1. **Fake relationships can reveal real feelings** — Proximity fosters connection.
2. **Imposter syndrome affects personal life too** — Career pressures shape identity.
3. **Communication builds intimacy** — Emotional honesty drives the resolution.

## Best for

Fans of contemporary romance with lighthearted tone and strong character chemistry. Particularly appealing to readers interested in academic settings.
