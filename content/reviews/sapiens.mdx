*Sapiens* traces the evolution of Homo sapiens from small hunter-gatherer groups to dominant global species. Our review finds it intellectually stimulating and highly accessible despite its ambitious scope.

## Why we recommend it

Harari connects biology, anthropology, economics, and politics into a single narrative. The book challenges conventional assumptions about progress, happiness, and civilization.

## Key takeaways

1. **Shared myths enable large societies** — Religion, money, and nations rely on collective belief.
2. **Agricultural progress came with trade-offs** — Stability often replaced freedom.
3. **Technological growth outpaces ethical development** — Power increases faster than wisdom.

## Best for

Readers curious about big-picture human history and interdisciplinary insights into society.
