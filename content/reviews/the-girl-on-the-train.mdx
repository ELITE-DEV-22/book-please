This novel follows Rachel, an alcoholic divorcee who becomes entangled in a missing-person case after observing a seemingly perfect couple from her daily train commute. Our review highlights its focus on memory, perception, and obsession.

## Why we recommend it

The story unfolds through multiple perspectives, each revealing conflicting versions of events. Rachel’s unreliable memory creates suspense while exploring themes of addiction, loneliness, and voyeurism. The suburban setting enhances the sense that danger lurks beneath ordinary life.

## Key takeaways

1. **Memory is fragile and subjective** — Truth emerges slowly and painfully.
2. **Obsession can distort reality** — Rachel’s fixation drives the narrative.
3. **Domestic spaces hide secrets** — Familiar environments become threatening.

## Best for

Fans of psychological suspense with flawed protagonists. A strong pick for readers who enjoy character-driven mysteries over action-heavy thrillers.
