This book tells the story of Henrietta Lacks, whose cancer cells were taken without consent and became one of the most important tools in medical research. Our review finds it both informative and emotionally powerful.

## Why we recommend it

Skloot combines scientific explanation with personal narrative, highlighting ethical questions surrounding medical research, consent, and inequality.

## Key takeaways

1. **Scientific progress can involve ethical dilemmas** — Advancement and justice may conflict.
2. **Individual stories humanize science** — Data gains meaning through context.
3. **Recognition matters** — Contributions often go unacknowledged.

## Best for

Readers interested in medical history, ethics, and science told through compelling storytelling.
